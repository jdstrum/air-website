For Entertainment Purposes Only

Ryan Serhant almost lost a $50 million deal because both sides asked ChatGPT. The seller asked if the price was fair. ChatGPT said they were leaving money on the table. The buyer asked if they were overpaying. ChatGPT said yes. Same deal. Same price. The tool told both of them to walk away.

Only days ago, TechCrunch reported that Microsoft's own terms of service for Copilot includes this line in bold capital letters: "Copilot is for entertainment purposes only." Six months of selling an enterprise productivity tool while the legal page says it's a toy.
