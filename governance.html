What governance actually looks like.

AI tool visibility

A complete picture of which AI tools are in your environment, which are sanctioned, and which showed up on their own.

Data classification

Clear rules for what data can go where. What's safe for public AI tools, what stays internal, and what never leaves the building.

Acceptable use policies

Operational guidelines employees actually follow because they're clear, specific, and built around how people already work.

Shadow AI detection

The tools you didn't approve are already in your environment. Governance finds them so you can decide what to do about them.

Output verification

AI generates confident output whether the information is accurate or not. Governance defines what gets human review before it goes out the door.

Enablement, not restriction

Approved tool paths, clear escalation, fast tracks for governed innovation. The goal is policies that say yes more than they say no.

The Opportunity

Every time your team uses a public AI tool, intelligence leaves the building.

Your employees are using ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and Claude to draft strategies, analyze data, write proposals, and solve problems every day. That thinking is your company's intellectual capital. Without a secure AI gateway, it disappears into public models and you never see it again.

Capture institutional knowledge

A governed gateway routes AI usage through your environment. The intelligence your team creates stays yours and compounds over time.

Visibility into how AI is being used

See what's being asked, what's being generated, and where the value is coming from. You can't optimize what you can't see.

Data stays inside the perimeter

Sensitive information, customer data, financials, IP. A gateway ensures none of it reaches public models.

AI becomes a business asset

The difference between AI as a cost center and AI as an asset is whether you're capturing the value it creates.
