The five capabilities that separate AI adoption from AI exposure.

01Data classification+

Which data can AI tools touch and which can't? Without clear classification, proprietary information flows into public models every time someone writes a prompt.

02Acceptable use policies+

What are your people allowed to do with AI, and what's off limits? Most companies have no documented answer. That means every employee is making their own rules.

03Shadow AI detection+

Your employees are already using AI tools you haven't sanctioned. The question is whether you know which ones, what data is going in, and what's coming out.

04Output verification+

AI hallucination is real — confident, polished, and wrong. Without verification processes, hallucinated content enters your operations unchecked.

05Enablement paths+

Governance isn't just about saying no. It's about giving people a clear way to say yes — approved tools, sanctioned workflows, and training that turns cautious adopters into confident ones.
