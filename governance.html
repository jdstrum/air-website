The Governance Gap

Every time your team uses a public AI tool, intelligence leaves the building.
Governance is how you get it back.

Get Started

The five capabilities that separate AI adoption from AI exposure.

01Data classification+

Which data can AI tools touch and which can't? Without clear classification, proprietary information flows into public models every time someone writes a prompt.

02Acceptable use policies+

What are your people allowed to do with AI, and what's off limits? Most companies have no documented answer. That means every employee is making their own rules.

03Shadow AI detection+

Your employees are already using AI tools you haven't sanctioned. The question is whether you know which ones, what data is going in, and what's coming out.

04Output verification+

AI hallucination is real — confident, polished, and wrong. Without verification processes, hallucinated content enters your operations unchecked.

05Enablement paths+

Governance isn't just about saying no. It's about giving people a clear way to say yes — approved tools, sanctioned workflows, and training that turns cautious adopters into confident ones.

The Opportunity

Governance is how you secure your AI environment — and compound every advantage it creates.

Most mid-market companies have AI usage without AI governance — proprietary data flowing into public models, insights that never come back, zero visibility. The organizations closing that gap aren't just reducing risk. They're building a compounding advantage.

Intelligence compounds

Every AI interaction generates organizational intelligence — solutions, insights, patterns. A governed environment captures it all, turning daily work into a strategic asset that gets smarter over time.

Move from guessing to steering

Know exactly where AI is being used, by whom, and with what data. Visibility turns unmanaged AI sprawl into a portfolio you can measure, direct, and scale.

Data becomes your moat

When your proprietary data stays in your environment, it becomes a training ground for competitive advantage. The companies that protect their data aren't just safer — they're building something no one else can replicate.

Confidence to say yes

When governance is in place, leadership stops blocking AI initiatives and starts enabling them. Clear policies and guardrails mean teams can adopt new tools without a risk review bottleneck on every request.
