Where do you actually stand?

This assessment measures your organization across the Six Dimensions of AI Readiness. You'll see where you're strong, where you have gaps, and exactly what moving the needle will require.

Strategic Vision & Leadership

Does your organization have a clear, documented AI vision that connects to business outcomes? Are your executive team and board aligned on how AI will create value?

What moves the needle: Executive alignment on AI strategy, connected to quarterly business metrics. A governance model that enables speed.

Data Readiness

Can your organization actually use its data? Is it clean, classified, and accessible to the models and workflows that need it? Or is your data siloed and duplicated across systems?

What moves the needle: A data strategy that starts with business questions, not technology. Clear ownership and quality standards.

Technology Infrastructure

Can your current infrastructure handle AI workloads? Do you have the compute, the APIs, the integrations? Or does every AI tool require manual workarounds and duct-tape solutions?

What moves the needle: Consolidated cloud strategy and API-first architecture. Simplified integrations that don't require custom code.

Governance & Security

Do you have policies in place for how AI can be used? Do you know what data is being exposed to public models? Does your legal and compliance team understand your AI posture?

What moves the needle: Documented AI policies, an audit framework, clear data classification. A governance model that enables innovation, not just gates it.

Customer Experience Readiness

Have you identified where AI creates measurable customer value? Do your product and service teams have a roadmap for AI-enabled customer experiences? Can you operationalize them?

What moves the needle: Customer research linked to AI capabilities. Product roadmaps with clear success metrics. The ability to learn and iterate.

Workforce & Culture Readiness

Do your teams understand what AI can do in their roles? Are they equipped to use it? Or are they worried about being replaced, resisting adoption, or simply unaware?

What moves the needle: Clear communication about what AI is and isn't. Role-based training. Recognition and reward for teams that embrace and scale AI wins.
